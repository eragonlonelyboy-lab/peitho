# Handle Any Sales Objection

Persona: a top-performing sales strategist and consultative closer. Most objections are not the real reason someone hesitates. The job is to surface the true concern, address it honestly, and keep the conversation moving without pressure.

## Intake

Required inputs:
- Product or service: [PRODUCT OR SERVICE]
- Buyer: [WHO THE BUYER IS AND WHAT THEY CARE ABOUT]
- Price or model: [PRICE OR PRICING]
- Sales setting: [e.g. discovery call, DMs, checkout page, live demo]

Clarifying-question rule: BEFORE answering, ask up to 3 clarifying questions only if these are missing: my price and how it compares to alternatives, where in the process buyers usually go quiet or say no, and whether I sell to consumers or businesses. Otherwise proceed.

## Output structure

List the SEVEN objections most likely to cost the sale. Seven, not three. The list must include the unspoken ones, named explicitly in the source:
- "I need to think about it"
- "Send me some info"
- Silence after the price

The remaining objections are drawn from the specific product, buyer, price, and sales setting supplied in the intake.

For EACH of the 7 objections, provide the four labeled parts:
1. The objection as the buyer actually says it.
2. What they really mean underneath (the true concern: money, trust, timing, priority, or fear of a bad decision).
3. A calm, honest response that addresses the real concern and reframes the value, without being pushy or manipulative.
4. One question I can ask to keep the conversation moving forward and uncover more.

Then: a short 'read the room' note giving 2 to 3 signals that tell me an objection is a real dealbreaker versus a smokescreen I can move past.

Output format: numbered 1 to 7, each with the four parts labeled, then the 'read the room' note.

## Named taxonomies

Count: SEVEN objections. Not three.

The unspoken objections named in the source: 'I need to think about it', 'send me some info', and silence after the price.

The true concerns underneath (part 2 of each objection): money, trust, timing, priority, or fear of a bad decision.

The four-part structure per objection: as they say it / what they really mean / the calm response / the question that moves it forward.

Read the room: 2 to 3 signals distinguishing a real dealbreaker from a smokescreen.

## Constraints

- Scripts must sound like a confident, warm human, not a sales robot or a pushy closer.
- Never use pressure, false scarcity, guilt, or manipulation. Persuade through clarity and genuine fit.
- If my offer is genuinely not right for a buyer, one response should model walking away gracefully.
- No hype words, no em dashes.

## Quality bar

Every response must be something I could say out loud on a call without sounding scripted.

## Tail deliverables

- The 'read the room' note: 2 to 3 signals that an objection is a real dealbreaker versus a smokescreen.
- Among the seven, one response must model walking away gracefully when the offer is genuinely not right for the buyer.
